How to play & the strategy behind the three dynamics

Each turn you collect Gold equal to your Economy, then take one action (or Skip to just bank the gold). The enemy AI does the same. First to knock the opponent's Base from 30 to 0 wins. Attacks unlock on turn 4 and cost 3 gold to launch.

Economy is the engine — more gold every turn, forever (you'll see farms appear as it grows). But a turn spent on economy is a turn not spent defending, so a pure economy player is exposed once attacks open up.

Military is the only way to deal damage, capped at 15 troops. Three types, each with attack/defense stats: Infantry (3g, 2/2 — balanced), Archers (3g, 3/1 — hit hard but fragile on defense), Knights (6g, 4/4 — elite, durable). When armies clash, the attacker's attack meets the defender's defense; defenders have the edge, and only overflow reaches the Base — so composition matters.

Research comes in two tracks you choose between: +Attack (adds attack to every troop) and +Defense (adds defense to every troop). Pour into Attack to crack a turtle, or Defense to harden against a rush.

The balance: Economy beats a slow opponent, aggression beats greedy economy, and Research beats a big-but-primitive army. Mass Archers to break a turtle, or hold Knights to repel a rush. Set the AI difficulty up top — changing it starts a fresh duel.
