How to play & the strategy behind the three dynamics

Each turn you collect Gold equal to your Economy, then take one action (or Skip to just bank the gold). The enemy does the same. First to knock the opponent's Base from 30 to 0 wins. Attacks unlock on turn 4 and cost 3 gold to launch.

Modes (top of the screen): vs AI plays against the computer at your chosen difficulty, or 2 Players is local hotseat — two people share one device and the shared controls drive whoever's turn it is. Between turns a "your turn" screen lets you pass the device. Switching mode starts a fresh duel.

Economy is the engine — more gold every turn (you'll see farms appear as it grows), up to a maximum of +15/turn. But a turn spent on economy is a turn not spent defending, so a pure economy player is exposed once attacks open up.

Military is the only way to deal damage, capped at 15 troops. Three types, each with attack/defense stats: Infantry (3g, 2/2 — balanced), Archers (3g, 3/1 — hit hard but fragile on defense), Knights (6g, 4/4 — elite, durable). When armies clash, the attacker's attack meets the defender's defense; defenders have the edge, and only overflow reaches the Base.

Counter triangle: each unit deals +30% attack when it attacks the type it beats — Infantry › Knights › Archers › Infantry (defense is raw, so counters reward picking the right attacker, not reacting last). Watch the enemy's army on their field and bring the unit that counters their main force. The AI reads your army too and counters it — and it will rush you if you over-greed economy, so vary your play.

Research comes in two tracks you choose between: +Attack (adds attack to every troop) and +Defense (adds defense to every troop). You get 5 research points total across both tracks (e.g. 4 attack + 1 defense) — spend them to crack a turtle or harden against a rush.

Boost Attack is a one-time trump card (you and the AI each get one): for 12 gold it gives +40% attack on your next 2 turns. Activating it uses your turn, so time it right — pop it, then attack into a defense you couldn't otherwise break. Pick a troop type and quantity, then press the Train button to commit (nothing trains until you do).

The balance: Economy beats a slow opponent, aggression beats greedy economy, and Research beats a big-but-primitive army. Mass Archers to break a turtle, or hold Knights to repel a rush. Set the AI difficulty up top — changing it starts a fresh duel.
